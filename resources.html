Tips for Preventing Theft

Secure belongings:

  • Keep valuable items like phones, laptops, and wallets out of plain sight or locked in a secure place.
  • Use anti-theft bags or backpacks with hidden zippers if carrying valuable items.

Stay Aware of Surroundings:

  • Pay attention to your environment, especially in crowded or unfamiliar areas.
  • If you notice suspicious behavior, don’t ignore it—move to a safer location or alert someone nearby.

Community Awareness and Involvement:

  • Coordinate with others, such as classmates, coworkers, or neighbors, to establish a "watch system."
  • Encourage everyone to look out for each other’s belongings and report suspicious activity.

Tips for reporting incidents

Document Details:

  • Note down important information about the incident: time, date, location, and any identifiable details about the thief or suspicious person.
  • If possible, take pictures or record relevant details without putting yourself at risk.

Report Immediately:

  • Report theft or suspicious activity as soon as possible to campus security, local authorities, or store management.
  • For lost or stolen bank cards, phones, or other critical items, contact the respective institutions immediately to disable access.

Spread Awareness:

  • Share the incident within your community or workplace if appropriate. This can help prevent future incidents and prompt others to secure their belongings.
